About Us

The E&W Manager as of right now is a webapp that makes people aware of saving power, water, and yes of course money. As of right now our webapp has very helpful tips and information on how to save power, water, and money. On our webapp we have a calculator that calculates how much water and power you use by simply answering a few questions, in the end of your calculation the calculator totals everything up and gives you the total of how much water, power, and money you spend for certain everyday things. This calculator can help people learn new ways to save and make our planet better.

Tools and Tips

We can help you by providing an estimation calculator on your bills on either your power or water bills. we can also give tips on how you can save water or electricity.A tip for example, is unplugging your devices or turning them off when you leave the house.

Tips

Click here to see Water Tips

 

Water

  1. Fix broken faucets in your house to save water.

    - Bye fixing brocken faucets you can stop leaks, saving you money.

  2. Turn off the water when brushing your teeth, washing your face, shaving, etc

    - If you turn off the water in between brushing your teeth you can save an estimate of about a gallon of water

  3. Take shorter showers! This will save between 5 and 10 gallons of water per minute.

    - Thats a lot of water per minute you can save it!!!

  4. Don't flush any type of garbage down the toilet. Every flush uses around 7 gallons of water!

    - Imagen how much water that is, 7 gallons!!!, use it wisely.

  5. When washing dishes, use a full sink of warm water instead of running the faucet continuously.

    - You can save water like this because you can fill it once and you don't need to leave the water on or turning it on and off.

  6. Check your toilets for leaks

    - Put a little food coloring in your toilet tank. If, without flushing, the color begins to appear in the bowl within 30 minutes, you have a leak that should be repaired immediately. Most replacement parts are inexpensive and easy to install.

  7. Put plastic bottles or float booster in your toilet tank

    - To cut down on water waste, put an inch or two of sand or pebbles inside each of two plastic bottles to weigh them down. Fill the bottles with water, screw the lids on, and put them in your toilet tank, safely away from the operating mechanisms. Or, buy an inexpensive tank bank or float booster. This may save ten or more gallons of water per day.

  8. Rinse your razor in the sink

    - Fill the sink with a few inches of warm water. This will rinse your razor just as well as running water, with far less waste of water.

  9. Don't water the gutter

    - Position your sprinklers so water lands on the lawn or garden, not on paved areas. Also, avoid watering on windy days.

Power

  1. Turn off lights when nobody is in the room.

    - Turning off the lights in a room while nobody is in it can save lots of electricity.

  2. Unplug devices from outlets such as: DVD players, TV, etc.

    - Leaving devices plugged in can still consume power even if its not in use.

  3. Install fluorescent lighting to save on electricity bill.

    - Fluorescent lighting can save you an estimate of $80 or more per year.

  4. Do a home energy audit. This survey analyzes your home's structure, appliances and insulation, as well as your family's lifestyle. Alliant Energy offers customers a free online energy audit called My Home Comfort Check Up that provides a personalized report detailing specific ways to save energy throughout your home. Heating your home

  5. Change or clean your furnace filter once a month. Dust and dirt can quickly clog vital parts, making your furnace run harder and eventually break down.

  6. Have your heating system inspected regularly - especially if it's natural gas. A $50-100 annual tune-up can help reduce your heating costs by up to five percent.

  7. If you have a forced-air furnace, do NOT close heat registers in unused rooms. Your furnace is designed to heat a specific square footage of space and can't sense a register is closed - it will continue working at the same pace. In addition, the cold air from unheated rooms can escape into the rest of the house, reducing the effectiveness of all your insulating and weatherizing.

  8. Install a programmable thermostat. If you use it to set back the temperature by 10 degrees for eight hours every night, you'll lower your heating bills by 10 percent. A $50 digital thermostat can pay for itself in energy savings in less than a year.

  9. Don't set the thermostat higher than you actually want it. It won't heat your home any faster, and it will keep your furnace running longer than necessary.

  10. Vacuum registers and vents regularly, and don't let furniture and draperies block the air flow. Inexpensive plastic deflectors can direct air under tables and chairs.

  11. If your home has a boiler system, avoid covering radiators with screens or blocking them with furniture. It's also a good idea to add a reflecting panel behind radiators - you can purchase one at a home center or make one yourself with a plywood panel and aluminum foil.

  12. If your home has electric baseboard heating, be sure to keep furniture and draperies away from the heaters, and leave at least a three-inch clearance under the heating unit.

  13. Keep curtains and blinds closed at night to keep cold air out, but open them during the day to let the sun warm the room.

  14. Avoid using space heaters, including electric, kerosene or propane models. Not only are they expensive to operate, they're also very dangerous.

  15. If you have hardwood or tile floors, add area rugs to keep your feet warm.

  16. If you'll be going on vacation, lower the thermostat to 55 degrees. This will save energy while preventing water pipes from freezing.

  17. If you have a wood-burning fireplace, have the chimney cleaned and inspected regularly, and burn only fully dried hardwoods to produce the most heat output.
